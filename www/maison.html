Roger Vivier – the brand credited with inventing the stiletto – has a long and rich history. dive into vivier style and find out about the milestones of the maison roger vivier.

EARLY DAYS

Born in 1907 Roger Vivier created shoes like sculptures; he was called the Fabergé of shoes. In 1924, he began studying at the Beaux Arts of Paris (sculpture course) which he abandoned two years later for shoemaking and started with an apprenticeship. Fascinated by theater and music halls, Vivier designed theater decors. Backstage, he encountered Josephine Baker and Mistinguette for whom he began designing custom made shoes.

THE ARTISAN

Following the success of his models he opened in 1937 his own boutique rue Royale in Paris. Heels were his field of excellence from the Stiletto he was the first to launch in 1954 to the sinuous Virgule, intended as the manifesto for his own label that made its début in 1963. Virtuose artisan, passionate about feminine elegance, he raised the shoe to the rank of works of art thanks to the traditional know-how of French embroidery Houses such as Rébéor Lesage.

THE PASSION OF SHOES

Posterity will also remember Roger Vivier for a list of exceptional women. Not only did he create the shoes for the coronation of Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II in 1953, he also put shoes on the greatest female figures of their times: the Duchess of Windsor, Princess Soraya of Iran, Marlène Dietrich, Elizabeth Taylor, Brigitte Bardot and of course Catherine Deneuve in “Belle de Jour”, when the shoe’s vamp was signed by the famous silver Buckle.

THE CHALLENGE

In 2004 Bruno Frisoni, creative director, took on the challenge of turning the famous name into a luxury brand, renowned for its accessories.

THE ARTISAN

A creative step which, nourished by the historic and artistic richness of the brand, allows for infinite forms of interpretation. Bringing Roger Vivier back to the present to render it more accessible to a woman who likes to blend elegance and efficiency, Frisoni has also preserved the essence of the brand itself – sophistication and eccentricity – from head to toe.
